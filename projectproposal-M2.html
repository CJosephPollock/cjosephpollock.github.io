Joe Pollock's Portfolio

My website will be my personal website of my work that I could add onto a resume or onto business cards, etc. Thus, the site title, "Joe Pollock's Portfolio".

Developer

I want this website to demonstrate my knowledge and skill about web dev, and iOS development, so I will be the only developer on this project because of that.

Rationale or Focus

It will focus on work I have done and projects I am planning. It will give the visitor (potential employer) a good idea of my skill level and passion for coding and development.

Main Elements Outline

The website will be very clean with no ads, no clutter, and will be extremely responsive and fast. There will be a title at the top that says, "Joe Pollock's Portfolio" with a nav bar including pages such as, Home, about, projects, resume, contact, etc. There will be a footer with an © 2014, a "to the top" button, and some social media links (twitter, linked in, github, email, etc.)

Content

There will probably be about 6 pages on my website. I am still deciding whether to break up each "Project" into a different page, or just have a page that scrolls down to accomodate each project. So in that case, there will be about 6 (not breaking the projects up) to 10 (giving each project its own page).

Target audience

My target audience will be a potential employer, someone looking for freelance work, or even a potential partner.

Design Considerations

I want the site to be very clean and modern. I like the use of blurry backgrounds on these websites. It will not be clunky or have extraneous information that is unnecessary.

Limiting Factors

I think content at this point is the limiting factor. I have a few ideas for some quick and easy iPhone apps to quickly demonstrate what I know. I am about to be on summer vacation, so I will have time to quickly build those and set up their project pages. Other than that, just writing compelling content and optimizing the site!
